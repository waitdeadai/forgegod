4. Legal Basis for Processing (Article 6 GDPR)

We process personal data only when we have a valid legal basis under Article 6 of the GDPR. The following are the legal bases on which we rely:

4.1 Contract Performance (Art. 6(1)(b))

Processing is necessary for the performance of a contract to which you are party, or to take steps at your request prior to entering into a contract. This includes processing for:

  • Providing and delivering our consulting services as specified in Statements of Work
  • Managing the client relationship, including communication regarding project status and deliverables
  • Processing payments and issuing invoices for services rendered
  • Providing post-engagement support and documentation

4.2 Legitimate Interests (Art. 6(1)(f))

Processing is necessary for the purposes of our legitimate interests, provided that such interests are not overridden by your fundamental rights and freedoms. Our legitimate interests include:

  • Operating and improving our business operations, including internal accounting and administrative functions
  • Detecting and preventing fraud, security threats, and unauthorized access
  • Establishing, exercising, or defending legal claims or proceedings
  • Direct marketing of similar consulting services to existing clients (with an opt-out mechanism)
  • Ensuring network and information security, including access controls and audit logging

4.3 Consent (Art. 6(1)(a))

Processing is based on your freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent. Consent is obtained for:

  • Marketing communications beyond existing client relationships
  • Storing non-essential cookies on our website (see Section 10)
  • Processing of special categories of data where required

You may withdraw your consent at any time by contacting us at legal@smoothrevenue.com or using the unsubscribe mechanism in marketing emails. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out prior to withdrawal.

4.4 Legal Obligation (Art. 6(1)(c))

Processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation to which we are subject, including:

  • Tax and accounting record-keeping requirements
  • Anti-money laundering and export control compliance
  • Responding to lawful requests from competent public authorities

6. Data Subject Rights (Articles 15-22 GDPR)

You have the following rights with respect to your personal data. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at legal@smoothrevenue.com or dpo@smoothrevenue.com. We will respond to your request within one month of receipt.

Right of Access (Art. 15)

Obtain confirmation as to whether we process your personal data and access to that data, including the purposes of processing, categories of data, recipients, and retention periods.

Right to Rectification (Art. 16)

Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data concerning you, without undue delay.

Right to Erasure (Art. 17)

Request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, you withdraw consent, or processing is unlawful.

Right to Restriction (Art. 18)

Request restriction of processing in certain circumstances, such as when you contest data accuracy or object to processing.

Right to Portability (Art. 20)

Receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and transmit it to another controller where processing is based on consent or contract.

Right to Object (Art. 21)

Object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling for direct marketing purposes. We will cease processing unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds.

6.1 Right to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe that our processing of your personal data violates GDPR requirements, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. For residents of European Union member states, you may contact the supervisory authority in your country of residence. For Spanish residents, see Section 13 below.

6.2 Automated Decision-Making

We do not use automated decision-making, including profiling, that produces legal effects concerning you or similarly significant effects. In the event that we implement such processing in the future, you will be notified in advance and have the right to request human intervention.

7. Retention Periods

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, unless a longer retention period is required by law. The following retention periods apply:

7.1 Contract and Billing Data

Contract documents, Statements of Work, invoices, and payment records are retained for three (3) years following the end of the calendar year in which the contract was completed or terminated, in accordance with applicable statute of limitations and tax record requirements.

7.2 Inquiry and Pre-Contract Data

Personal data collected through website inquiry forms, initial consultation requests, and pre-contract communications is retained for one (1) year from the date of last contact, unless a contract is subsequently entered into, in which case the contract retention period applies.

7.3 Marketing Data

Personal data used for marketing purposes is retained until you withdraw consent or object to processing. Upon withdrawal, your data is deleted within thirty (30) days. We maintain a suppression list to ensure no further marketing contact is made.

7.4 Technical and Usage Data

Server logs and technical usage data collected via cookies is retained for a maximum of twelve (12) months, except where a longer period is required for security incident investigation.

7.5 Legal Compliance Data

Personal data necessary for compliance with legal obligations, including tax records and anti-money laundering documentation, is retained for the periods required by applicable law (typically five to seven years).

Upon expiration of the applicable retention period, personal data is securely deleted, anonymized, or archived in accordance with our data retention procedures.

10. Cookie Policy

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website smoothrevenue.com. Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website.

10.1 Essential Cookies

We use essential cookies that are strictly necessary for the functioning of our website. These cookies do not require your consent as they are necessary to provide the core functionality of the site (e.g., security features, session management). These cookies cannot be disabled without causing the website to malfunction.

10.2 Functional Cookies

We use functional cookies that enhance your experience by remembering your preferences (e.g., language settings, dark mode preference). These cookies are optional but help provide a more personalized experience.

10.3 No Tracking or Advertising Cookies

We do not use:

  • Tracking cookies that monitor your browsing behavior across websites
  • Advertising cookies or targeted advertising cookies
  • Third-party cookies from advertising networks
  • Cookies for building user profiles for commercial purposes

10.4 Cookie Management

You can manage your cookie preferences through your web browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies. Please note that blocking essential cookies may impair website functionality. For more information about managing cookies, visit your browser's help documentation.

10.5 Third-Party Embeds

Our website may contain embedded content (e.g., videos, social media posts) that sets its own cookies. We do not control these third-party cookies, and you should review the privacy policies of those third parties for information about their cookie practices.

11. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, loss, alteration, or destruction. Our security measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Encryption of sensitive data at rest using AES-256
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
  • Employee training on data protection and security awareness
  • Incident response procedures for data breach notification
  • Business continuity and disaster recovery capabilities

While we strive to protect your personal data, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security, but we are committed to maintaining appropriate security measures proportionate to the risks involved.
